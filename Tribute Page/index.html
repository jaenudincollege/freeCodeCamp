Ir. Soekarno

The Proclaimer of Indonesian Independence

Here's a timeline of Ir. Soekarno's life:

  • 1901 - Born on June 6 in Surabaya, East Java
  • 1921 - Moves to Bandung to study at the Technische Hoogeschool (now ITB), where he is influenced by nationalist ideas
  • 1926 - Graduates with an engineering degree, becoming "Ir. Soekarno"
  • 1927 - Founds the Indonesian National Party (PNI), advocating for independence from Dutch colonial rule
  • 1929 - Arrested by the Dutch authorities for his political activities, jailed for nearly two years
  • 1931 - Released from prison, continues to promote independence despite frequent arrests and exiles
  • 1942 - During World War II, negotiates with Japanese occupiers to gain support for Indonesian independence
  • 1945 - Proclaims Indonesiaâ€™s independence on August 17, alongside Mohammad Hatta, following the Japanese surrender
  • 1949 - After four years of struggle, the Dutch recognize Indonesia's independence
  • 1950 - Becomes the first President of Indonesia, emphasizing national unity and implementing Pancasila as the state philosophy
  • 1955 - Hosts the Bandung Conference, uniting Asian and African nations against colonialism and establishing the Non-Aligned Movement
  • 1960 - Advocates for Guided Democracy, aiming to stabilize and develop Indonesia amidst Cold War tensions
  • 1965 - Survives an attempted coup, leading to political turmoil and his eventual removal from power in 1967
  • 1970 - Dies on June 21, under house arrest, leaving a legacy of unwavering dedication to Indonesian sovereignty
